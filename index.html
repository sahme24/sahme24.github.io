About Me

So, who am I?

I'm Salman Ahmed, a Computer Science student at Carleton University doing my Infrastructure Co-op at the Department of National Defence. I hold a Level II Secret Clearance and spend most of my time in the space between cloud security, network defense, and vulnerability assessment.

I've built tools that scan cloud environments for misconfigurations, analyzed real network traffic with SIEM pipelines, and written embedded C++ running on mission-critical rocket hardware. If it involves protecting systems or pushing boundaries, I'm in.

Outside of work I've ridden camels, held a falcon, and I'm still the one in the group chat asking if the Wi-Fi is secure. Some things you just can't turn off.
